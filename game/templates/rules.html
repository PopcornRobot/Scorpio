A murderer walks in...

Ladies and gentlemen, an hour ago, there was a murder down the street. The suspects fled on foot and the police followed them here. They are relying on us to find them.

 

How the game works

This game is like a modified game of Mafia. But instead of eliminating the end, we vote who the murderers are. To find the murderers, we rely on informants to reveal real information that lead to real people.

It’s your job to talk to each other and find out who the murderers are.

This game is like a modified game of Mafia. In one hour, we will vote on who the murderers are. To find the murderers, everyone is given an Orb of Truth (golf ball).

These orbs reveal a fact about the murderer. Real facts. Facts like “this person has been skydiving" that lead to real people. It’s your job to talk to each other and find out who the murderers are. The number of murderers in the room will be revealed when roles are assigned.

 

Roles

Informant

  1. Goal - Find the murderers and vote them out at the end of the game.
  2. Powers - Each informant is given an Orb of Truth. Secretly deliver your orb to the Chief of Police so they can reveal a tip on who the murderer is.
  3. Weakness - The Orb of Truth protects you from harm. Once given up, the murderer can attack you.

 

 

Murderers

  1. Goal - Convince everyone you are not the murderer.
  2. Powers - Murderers cannot harm anyone holding an Orb of Truth. If the murderer sees an informant without an orb, they can kill them by pulling that informant aside and saying, "I'm the murderer. You're dead."
  3. Weakness - If a murderer tries to kill an informant holding an orb, the murderer dies and informant lives.

 

 

Chief of police

  1. Goal - Lead the group into finding the murderers and vote them out at the end of the game.

  2. Powers - The Chief is immortal and cannot be killed by murderers.

  3. Responsibilities - The Chief is responsible for two announcements:

    1. Tips - To unlock information about the murderers, informants will pass their Orbs of Truth to you. When you receive them, you will announce the tip to the public and destroy the tip and Orb forever. You can only hold one Orb at a time.
    2. Deaths - If a character dies, they will quietly inform you of their death and the role they played. You will announce it to the public. Feel free to be creative with this!

 

3 Cardinal Rules

  1. No lies. If someone asks you a question about your life, you must tell them the truth (within reasonable bounds of course). You can lie about who you think is the murderer.

  2. Don't be rude. Once a tip has been read (e.g. This person has been to Costco), it is rude to ask, “Have you been to Costco?” It implies you think that person is the murderer.

    The tips are real facts about the murderer. To figure out who went skydiving or costco, talk to people about their lives. Again, you cannot explicitly ask (e.g. Have you been to costco?), but you can implicitly ask (e.g. Do you buy on a weekly basis or in bulk?).

  3. Don't die. If you get killed, you must quietly inform the Chief of Police of your death and the character role you played. The Chief will announce it to the public.

    There are two ways to die:

    1. Informant dies. Murderer pulls an informant without an orb aside and says, "I'm the murderer. You're dead."
    2. Murderer dies. Murderer tries to kill an informant with an orb. Murderer dies. Informant remains in the game.

 

Summary

Our goal is to find the murderer. Everyone is given an Orb of Truth (golf ball). Each orb, when given to the Chief, reveals a fact about the murderer. Be careful though, once we give up our orb, we can be killed by the murderer.
